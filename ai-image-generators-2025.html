Top 5 AI Image Generators Compared: Which One Should You Use?

By Tools by Jarvis Team · Updated February 21, 2025 · 14 min read

The AI image generation space has matured dramatically. What was a novelty in 2023 is now a legitimate creative tool used by designers, marketers, content creators, and businesses worldwide. But with so many options available, choosing the right tool can be overwhelming.

We tested the five most popular AI image generators — DALL-E 3, Midjourney v6, Stable Diffusion XL, Ideogram 2.0, and Leonardo AI — using identical prompts across seven categories: photorealism, illustration, typography, product mockups, landscapes, portraits, and abstract art.

Our methodology: Each tool received the exact same 7 prompts. We evaluated output quality, prompt adherence, aesthetic appeal, text rendering, and consistency across multiple generations. All tests used default settings unless otherwise noted.

1. Midjourney v6 — The Artist's Choice

9.2/10

Midjourney remains the undisputed king of aesthetic quality. Version 6 brought significant improvements in photorealism, prompt understanding, and text rendering. If you care most about how your images look — and you're willing to learn the Discord-based workflow — Midjourney is the tool to beat.

Photorealism: Midjourney v6 produces the most convincing photorealistic images of any generator. Our golden retriever chef prompt yielded an image that could genuinely pass as a photograph at first glance. The lighting, depth of field, and material textures are remarkably natural.

Artistic quality: This is where Midjourney truly shines. Every image has a polished, almost editorial quality. The children's book illustration was charming and cohesive. The landscape had a cinematic quality reminiscent of professional nature photography. Even the abstract art felt intentional and balanced.

Typography: Version 6 dramatically improved text rendering, though it still struggles with longer phrases. Our "Morning Brew" sign came out beautifully — the lettering was stylish and integrated naturally into the image. But when we tested longer text (a full sentence), errors crept in about 40% of the time.

Speed & workflow: Here's the catch. Midjourney operates through Discord, which creates a learning curve for newcomers. You type commands in a chat interface, and your images generate alongside other users' creations. It works, but it's not intuitive. The web interface (alpha) is improving but still limited.

Pricing: No free tier. The Basic plan starts at $10/month for ~200 images. For serious users, the Standard plan ($30/month) with unlimited relaxed generations is the sweet spot.

2. DALL-E 3 (via ChatGPT) — The Easiest to Use

8.8/10

DALL-E 3 integrated into ChatGPT is the most accessible AI image generator available. You describe what you want in plain English — no prompt engineering required — and ChatGPT interprets your request, optimizes the prompt behind the scenes, and generates the image. It's almost absurdly easy to use.

Photorealism: DALL-E 3 produces excellent photorealistic images, though they sometimes have a slightly "clean" or "digital" quality compared to Midjourney. Our product mockup and portrait prompts yielded professional-grade results that would work for blog posts, social media, and presentations.

Prompt understanding: This is DALL-E 3's superpower. Because ChatGPT pre-processes your prompt, it understands complex, nuanced requests better than any competitor. You can say "make it more dramatic" or "change the color scheme to warm autumn tones" in a conversation, and it adjusts accordingly. No other tool matches this conversational iteration flow.

Typography: DALL-E 3 has decent text rendering — better than Stable Diffusion but worse than Ideogram. Our "Morning Brew" sign was readable and well-placed, but the font style wasn't as polished as Midjourney's version. Longer text works about 60% of the time.

The conversational advantage: Being embedded in ChatGPT means you can brainstorm, refine, and generate in one continuous conversation. "Generate a logo concept for a sustainable coffee brand" → "Make it more minimalist" → "Try it with earth tones" → "Now create 3 variations." This workflow is unmatched.

Free access: ChatGPT free users get limited DALL-E 3 generations (roughly 2-3 per day). ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) gets significantly more. It's not unlimited, but it's the only top-tier image generator with any free access at all.

3. Ideogram 2.0 — The Typography Champion

8.5/10

Ideogram made its name by doing what other AI image generators couldn't: rendering text accurately in images. Version 2.0 has expanded well beyond that niche, delivering strong overall image quality while maintaining its lead in typography. If your use case involves any text in images — logos, posters, social media graphics, signs — Ideogram should be your first choice.

Typography: Simply the best in the business. Our "Morning Brew" sign prompt produced a stunning image with perfectly rendered, stylistically appropriate text. We also tested it with a full paragraph of text on a book cover — Ideogram rendered it with about 95% accuracy, while competitors managed 40-60% at best. This alone makes it invaluable for graphic design work.

Overall image quality: Ideogram 2.0 is no longer just a text tool. The photorealism, while not quite matching Midjourney, is solid. Illustrations are attractive and well-composed. The model has a distinct aesthetic that's clean and commercially appealing — perfect for marketing materials.

Free tier: Ideogram offers one of the most generous free tiers: 25 images per day. That's approximately 750 images per month — far more than any competitor offers for free. For casual users and small businesses, this might be all you ever need.

Where it falls short: Artistic images (abstract art, painterly styles) feel less inspired than Midjourney. The model sometimes produces images that look slightly "stock photo-ish." The editing tools are basic compared to DALL-E 3's conversational approach.

4. Leonardo AI — The Game Artist's Dream

8.3/10

Leonardo AI carved out a unique position by excelling at concept art, game assets, and stylized imagery. While it handles photorealism decently, its real strength is in creating the kind of polished, stylized art you'd see in video games, fantasy books, and creative projects. It also offers the most sophisticated editing and fine-tuning tools of any generator on this list.

Stylized art: Leonardo produces stunning concept art and illustrations. Our children's book illustration was arguably the most charming of all five generators — it had character, warmth, and a distinct artistic style. The abstract art prompt also yielded impressive results with bold, intentional compositions.

Editing tools: This is Leonardo's secret weapon. The platform offers inpainting, outpainting, image-to-image transformation, and fine-tuned model selection. You can upload your own images, train custom models, and achieve a level of control that rivals Stable Diffusion — but with a friendly web interface.

Free tier: 150 tokens per day (roughly 15-30 images depending on settings). That's generous enough for daily experimentation. The tokens reset every 24 hours, so consistent users get meaningful value without paying.

Photorealism: Solid but not spectacular. Leonardo's photorealistic outputs sometimes have subtle artifacts that trained eyes will notice — slightly plastic-looking skin textures, or inconsistent lighting in complex scenes. For social media and web use, it's fine. For professional photography replacements, look elsewhere.

5. Stable Diffusion XL — The Open-Source Powerhouse

8.0/10

Stable Diffusion is the only fully open-source option on this list, and that changes everything. You can run it locally on your own hardware for free — unlimited generations, no subscriptions, no content filters, complete privacy. For developers, researchers, and power users, this freedom is worth more than any feature list.

Customization: No other tool comes close to Stable Diffusion's flexibility. With LoRA models, ControlNet, custom checkpoints, and an ecosystem of community-built tools, you can fine-tune the output to match virtually any style or requirement. Want a model trained specifically on your brand's aesthetic? Stable Diffusion is the only option that lets you do this locally and privately.

The learning curve: Let's be honest — Stable Diffusion is not beginner-friendly. Setting up a local installation requires technical knowledge (Python, CUDA, model management). Even with user-friendly interfaces like Automatic1111 or ComfyUI, there's a significant learning investment. Cloud-hosted versions (like DreamStudio) simplify this but lose some customization benefits.

Image quality: SDXL produces good images, but out-of-the-box quality trails Midjourney and DALL-E 3. The magic of Stable Diffusion lies in community models — the right checkpoint model combined with the right LoRA can produce images that rival or exceed any commercial tool. Finding that combination is part of the skill (and the fun).

Typography: The weakest of the five for text rendering. SDXL struggles with even short text, frequently producing garbled or misspelled words. This is a known limitation. If you need text in images, use Ideogram instead.

Hardware requirements: For local use, you'll need a GPU with at least 8GB VRAM (NVIDIA recommended). A modern gaming PC (RTX 3060 or better) handles SDXL comfortably. Mac users can run it via Apple Silicon, though generation is slower.

The Multi-Tool Approach

Here's what most experienced AI artists actually do: they use multiple tools. Start with DALL-E 3 in ChatGPT for quick brainstorming and concept exploration (it's the fastest way to iterate on ideas). Move to Midjourney for final, polished versions when quality matters most. Use Ideogram whenever text is involved. And keep Stable Diffusion for specialized tasks, custom styles, or batch generation.

This multi-tool workflow plays to each generator's strengths and costs less than you'd think. ChatGPT Plus ($20) gives you both DALL-E 3 and GPT-4. Midjourney Basic ($10) covers your polished outputs. Ideogram's free tier handles typography needs. Total: $30/month for access to the three best AI image generators on the market.

The Ethics Question

We'd be remiss not to address the ethical considerations. AI image generators were trained on vast datasets that included artists' work, often without consent. This remains a legitimate concern and an ongoing legal battle.

Our stance: Use AI image generators responsibly. Don't use them to replicate specific artists' styles without credit. Don't claim AI-generated images as hand-made. Be transparent about AI usage in professional contexts. Support human artists when you can — AI is a tool, not a replacement for human creativity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use AI-generated images commercially?

Yes, with caveats. Midjourney, DALL-E 3, Ideogram, and Leonardo all grant commercial usage rights on paid plans. Stable Diffusion's open license allows commercial use. However, always check the specific terms of service, and be aware that copyright law around AI-generated images is still evolving.

Which AI image generator is best for logos?

Ideogram for text-based logos, Midjourney for icon/symbol-based logos. However, we recommend using AI for logo brainstorming and then having a human designer refine the final version. AI-generated logos often need adjustment for scalability, color consistency, and brand guidelines.

Are AI-generated images copyrightable?

Currently, in the US, purely AI-generated images cannot be copyrighted. However, images with significant human creative input (selecting, editing, composing) may qualify. The legal landscape is evolving rapidly — consult a lawyer for specific cases.

Which is the fastest AI image generator?

DALL-E 3 (via ChatGPT) and Ideogram are the fastest at roughly 10-15 seconds per image. Midjourney takes 30-60 seconds. Stable Diffusion depends on your hardware (5 seconds to 2 minutes). Leonardo AI is moderate at 15-30 seconds.

Can I use AI image generators on my phone?

Yes. DALL-E 3 works through the ChatGPT mobile app. Midjourney works through the Discord mobile app. Ideogram and Leonardo AI have responsive web interfaces. Only Stable Diffusion requires a desktop (or cloud setup) for full functionality.
