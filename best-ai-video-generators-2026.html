Understanding the Two Types of AI Video

Before diving into individual reviews, it's important to understand that "AI video" means two very different things:

AI Avatar Videos (Synthesia, HeyGen): You write a script, choose a digital avatar, and the AI generates a realistic talking-head video. The avatar lip-syncs your script in any language. This is ideal for training videos, product demos, news-style content, and corporate communications.

Generative AI Video (Runway): You provide a text prompt or image, and the AI generates entirely new video footage. Think: cinematic clips, abstract visuals, B-roll footage, and creative content. This is closer to what Midjourney does for images, but for video.

These are fundamentally different tools for different purposes. Let's examine each in detail.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can AI video replace traditional video production?

For certain use cases, absolutely. Training videos, product demos, and simple explainers can be produced 10x faster and cheaper with AI. But for brand campaigns, emotional storytelling, and high-end production, traditional filmmaking still wins. Think of AI video as a complement, not a replacement.

Do AI-generated videos look fake?

It depends on the tool and use case. Synthesia's avatars are convincing enough for most corporate use. Runway's creative footage is often indistinguishable from stock video. But close inspection still reveals AI artifacts, especially in complex scenes with hands, text, or rapid movement.

What about deepfake concerns?

All reputable AI video tools have safeguards. Synthesia and HeyGen require consent verification for custom avatars, and generated videos include metadata identifying them as AI-created. Always use AI video ethically and disclose when content is AI-generated.
