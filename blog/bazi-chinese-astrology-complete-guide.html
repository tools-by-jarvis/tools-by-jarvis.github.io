Bazi Chinese Astrology: Complete Beginner's Guide to Four Pillars of Destiny

Published March 10, 2026 · 12 min read

Bazi (八字), also known as the Four Pillars of Destiny, is one of the most sophisticated systems of Chinese astrology. For over 1,000 years, it has been used to understand personality, predict life events, and guide important decisions. This comprehensive guide will teach you the fundamentals of Bazi from the ground up.

What Is Bazi?

Bazi literally means "eight characters" in Chinese. It refers to the eight Chinese characters that make up your birth chart, derived from the year, month, day, and hour of your birth. Each of these four time components is called a "pillar," hence the name Four Pillars of Destiny (四柱命理).

Unlike Western astrology, which focuses primarily on the positions of planets and stars, Bazi is rooted in the Chinese metaphysical concept of Yin-Yang and the Five Elements (Wu Xing). It provides a detailed map of your life's energetic blueprint — your strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and challenges.

The Building Blocks: Heavenly Stems and Earthly Branches

Every Bazi chart is built from two components:

The Ten Heavenly Stems (天干)

The Heavenly Stems represent the celestial influences and consist of ten characters, each associated with a Yin or Yang version of the Five Elements:

  • 甲 (Jiǎ) — Yang Wood: Like a tall tree, strong and upright
  • 乙 (Yǐ) — Yin Wood: Like a flower or vine, flexible and graceful
  • 丙 (Bǐng) — Yang Fire: Like the sun, warm, generous, and radiant
  • 丁 (Dīng) — Yin Fire: Like a candle flame, gentle and focused
  • 戊 (Wù) — Yang Earth: Like a mountain, stable and immovable
  • 己 (Jǐ) — Yin Earth: Like garden soil, nurturing and receptive
  • 庚 (Gēng) — Yang Metal: Like a sword, sharp, decisive, and strong
  • 辛 (Xīn) — Yin Metal: Like a jewel, refined and precious
  • 壬 (Rén) — Yang Water: Like an ocean, vast and powerful
  • 癸 (Guǐ) — Yin Water: Like morning dew, subtle and perceptive

The Twelve Earthly Branches (地支)

The Earthly Branches correspond to the twelve animals of the Chinese zodiac and represent terrestrial influences. Each branch also contains hidden Heavenly Stems, adding layers of complexity to chart interpretation:

  • 子 (Zǐ) — Rat | 丑 (Chǒu) — Ox | 寅 (Yín) — Tiger | 卯 (Mǎo) — Rabbit
  • 辰 (Chén) — Dragon | 巳 (Sì) — Snake | 午 (Wǔ) — Horse | 未 (Wèi) — Goat
  • 申 (Shēn) — Monkey | 酉 (Yǒu) — Rooster | 戌 (Xū) — Dog | 亥 (Hài) — Pig

The Five Elements (Wu Xing)

The Five Elements are the heart of Bazi analysis. They interact through cycles of production and control:

Production Cycle (相生): Wood feeds Fire → Fire creates Earth (ash) → Earth produces Metal (ore) → Metal carries Water (condensation) → Water nourishes Wood

Control Cycle (相剋): Wood controls Earth (roots) → Earth controls Water (dam) → Water controls Fire → Fire controls Metal (melting) → Metal controls Wood (axe)

Understanding these cycles is essential for interpreting how the elements in your chart interact with each other and with the elements of any given year, month, or day.

Reading Your Bazi Chart

Your Bazi chart consists of four pillars, each containing a Heavenly Stem on top and an Earthly Branch on the bottom:

  • Year Pillar: Represents your ancestry, early childhood, and social environment
  • Month Pillar: Represents your parents, career, and the strongest seasonal influence
  • Day Pillar: The most important pillar — the Heavenly Stem of the Day Pillar is your "Day Master," representing your core self
  • Hour Pillar: Represents your children, aspirations, and later life

The Day Master: Your Core Identity

The Day Master (日主) is the single most important element in your chart. It represents YOU. Everything else in the chart is interpreted in relation to your Day Master. For example, if your Day Master is Yang Wood (甲), then:

  • Wood elements represent your peers and competitors (比肩/劫財)
  • Fire elements represent your output and creativity (食神/傷官)
  • Earth elements represent your wealth and resources (正財/偏財)
  • Metal elements represent authority and pressure (正官/七殺)
  • Water elements represent your support and education (正印/偏印)

The Ten Gods (十神)

The relationship between your Day Master and every other element in your chart produces what Bazi practitioners call the "Ten Gods" — ten types of energetic relationships that describe different aspects of your life, including wealth, career, relationships, health, and creativity.

Luck Cycles (大運)

One of the most powerful features of Bazi is the concept of Luck Cycles (大運). These are 10-year periods that bring different elemental influences into your life. By analyzing your Luck Cycles, a Bazi practitioner can identify the best periods for career moves, relationships, investments, and major life changes.

Practical Applications of Bazi

  • Career guidance: Identify industries and roles that align with your elemental strengths
  • Relationship compatibility: Compare charts to assess romantic and business compatibility
  • Timing decisions: Choose favorable dates for launches, weddings, and investments
  • Health awareness: Identify potential health vulnerabilities based on elemental imbalances
  • Personal growth: Understand your natural tendencies and areas for development

Getting Started with Bazi

To explore your own Bazi chart, you'll need your exact birth date and time. Many free online calculators can generate your chart. However, interpreting a Bazi chart requires significant knowledge and experience. For beginners, we recommend starting with your Day Master element and gradually learning about the Ten Gods and element interactions.

Bazi is a lifelong study — even experienced practitioners continue to deepen their understanding. But even a basic knowledge of your chart can provide valuable insights into your personality, relationships, and life path.
