Chinese Name Analysis (姓名學) — How It Works

By Jarvis • March 9, 2026 • 11 min read

In Chinese culture, a name is far more than identification — it's considered a carrier of destiny. Chinese Name Analysis (姓名學, xìng míng xué) is a specialized branch of Chinese metaphysics that studies how the characters in a person's name influence their personality, fortune, health, and life trajectory. Parents across East Asia spend considerable time — and often consult naming specialists — to choose names that will bring their children favorable energy.

This guide explains the principles behind Chinese Name Analysis, the methods practitioners use, and why this tradition remains deeply influential in modern Chinese-speaking societies.

The Philosophy: Why Names Matter

Chinese metaphysics views the universe as an interconnected web of energy (氣, qi). Everything that exists — including sounds, symbols, and written characters — carries and transmits energy. Since a person's name is spoken and written thousands of times throughout their life, it creates a persistent energetic pattern that interacts with the person's innate destiny.

Think of it this way: your BaZi chart (birth chart) is your hardware — it's fixed at birth. Your name is part of your software — it can be configured to complement and enhance your hardware. A well-chosen name doesn't change your fundamental nature, but it can smooth rough edges, strengthen weaknesses, and amplify natural talents.

This isn't mere superstition to many Chinese families — it's a practical consideration on par with choosing the right school or neighborhood. Naming specialists (命名師) are consulted for newborns, business names, brand names, and even pen names.

The Five Grids Method (五格剖象法)

The most widely used Chinese Name Analysis system is the Five Grids Method, developed by Japanese scholar Kumazaki Kenō (熊崎健翁) in the early 20th century and later adopted extensively in the Chinese-speaking world. It analyzes a name through five numerical "grids" derived from the stroke counts of the name's characters.

How Stroke Counting Works

Each Chinese character has a specific number of strokes. In Name Analysis, practitioners use the traditional (繁體) stroke count even in regions that use simplified characters, because the traditional forms are considered to carry the original energetic value.

For example: the surname 陳 (Chen) has 16 strokes in its traditional form, even though the simplified version 陈 has only 10. Name Analysis always uses 16.

The Five Grids

A Chinese name typically has 2-3 characters: one surname character and one or two given name characters. The Five Grids are calculated as follows (for a three-character name with surname A and given name B + C):

1. Heaven Grid (天格): Surname strokes + 1. Represents inherited luck and family background. Since you can't change your surname, this grid is fixed and given less weight in analysis.

2. Personality Grid (人格): Surname strokes + first given name character strokes. This is the most important grid — it represents your core personality, temperament, and the overall direction of your life. The Personality Grid is the center of the analysis.

3. Earth Grid (地格): First given name strokes + second given name strokes. Represents your foundation, early life, and relationships. Influences youth, education, and social connections.

4. Outer Grid (外格): Total name strokes - Personality Grid strokes + 1. Represents your external environment, social relationships, and how others perceive you.

5. Total Grid (總格): Total strokes of all characters. Represents later life fortune, overall life achievement, and final outcomes.

Each grid's stroke count corresponds to a specific fortune pattern. The numbers 1-81 each have associated meanings and luck ratings. Some examples:

Five Elements in Name Analysis

Beyond numbers, each grid is associated with a Five Element based on its last digit:

  • Digits 1 or 2 → Wood
  • Digits 3 or 4 → Fire
  • Digits 5 or 6 → Earth
  • Digits 7 or 8 → Metal
  • Digits 9 or 0 → Water

The elements of the five grids should flow in a productive cycle — ideally, each grid's element produces or supports the next. The most critical relationship is between the Personality Grid and Earth Grid: they should be in a supportive relationship, not a destructive one.

Furthermore, the name's elements should complement the person's BaZi (birth chart). If a person's BaZi is deficient in Water, their name should include characters or grid elements associated with Water to compensate. This integration of Name Analysis with BaZi is what separates expert practitioners from simple stroke-counting algorithms.

Character Meaning and Energy

Beyond the numerical analysis, the actual meaning of name characters matters deeply in Chinese naming tradition:

Positive Character Choices

Characters are chosen for their meaning, sound, and visual balance. Popular naming characters and their associations:

  • 宇 (yǔ): Universe, vast — associated with ambition and big thinking
  • 慧 (huì): Wisdom, intelligence — associated with academic success
  • 安 (ān): Peace, safety — associated with stability and calm
  • 俊 (jùn): Handsome, talented — associated with excellence
  • 雅 (yǎ): Elegant, refined — associated with culture and grace
  • 翔 (xiáng): Soaring — associated with freedom and high achievement

Characters to Avoid

Names generally avoid characters with negative connotations, characters that are too common (reducing individuality), characters that are too obscure (causing practical difficulties), and characters whose sounds are homophones with unlucky words.

Sound Analysis (音韻)

The phonetic quality of a name also carries significance. A name should:

Flow naturally: The tones of the characters should create a pleasant, varied rhythm. Three characters all in the same tone sound flat and lack energy.

Avoid unfortunate homophones: In Mandarin, many characters share pronunciations. A name that sounds like an inauspicious word or phrase — even if the characters themselves are fine — is considered problematic.

Project the desired energy: Sharp, crisp sounds (like names beginning with "J" or "Z" sounds) project assertiveness. Softer sounds (like "Y" or "L" beginnings) project gentleness. The sound should match the intended personality expression.

Modern Applications

Baby Naming

In Taiwan, Hong Kong, and many overseas Chinese communities, consulting a name analyst for newborns is common practice. The process typically involves providing the baby's exact birth time (for BaZi analysis), receiving multiple name suggestions that balance stroke counts, elements, meaning, and sound, and the parents then choosing from the options based on personal preference.

Professional naming consultations cost anywhere from $50 to $500+, depending on the practitioner's reputation and the depth of analysis.

Business and Brand Names

Many Chinese businesses — from small shops to major corporations — have their company names analyzed for favorable stroke counts and elemental balance. The founder's BaZi is considered alongside the business name to ensure compatibility. Restaurant names, brand names, and product names all receive this treatment in markets where Chinese metaphysics is culturally embedded.

Name Changes

Adults who feel their current name is unfavorable sometimes adopt a new name — either legally or as a commonly-used alias. This is particularly common among entertainers, business people, and politicians in Chinese-speaking societies. The new name is chosen to address specific deficiencies identified in the original name's analysis.

Skepticism and Cultural Context

It's fair to ask: does changing a few strokes in your name really affect your life? From a strictly scientific perspective, there's no mechanism by which stroke counts could influence outcomes. Critics argue that Name Analysis is a combination of confirmation bias and cultural tradition.

However, dismissing it entirely misses important context. In cultures where Name Analysis is widely practiced, a name's perceived fortune can have real psychological and social effects. Confidence inspired by a "lucky" name is itself a form of luck. And the process of carefully, intentionally choosing a name — considering its sound, meaning, cultural resonance, and numerological properties — results in names that are genuinely beautiful and meaningful, regardless of metaphysical claims.

Whether you approach Chinese Name Analysis as a metaphysical practice or a cultural art form, it offers a fascinating lens into how Chinese civilization has thought about identity, language, and the hidden connections between symbols and destiny.

Getting Started with Name Analysis

If you want to analyze your Chinese name (or choose one), several free online tools can calculate the Five Grids and provide basic interpretations. For serious analysis — especially for baby naming or business naming — consulting a qualified practitioner who can integrate BaZi analysis provides far more nuanced and personalized guidance.

For those interested in Chinese metaphysics more broadly, Name Analysis pairs naturally with BaZi (Four Pillars) study, as both systems use the Five Elements framework and complement each other in practice.
